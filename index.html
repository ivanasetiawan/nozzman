Nozzman!

Introduction

If you're Dutch or living in the Netherlands, you most probably have seen some of his works sometime, somewhere unconsciously; Bram van Rijen, also known as Nozzman is a freelance graphic artist/illustrator/cartoonist and creative director at Mangrove in Rotterdam.

Some of his clients are NRC Handelsblad, nrc.next, Veronica Magazine, Moleskine, Erasmus University, NAi, ING, Zone 5300, Radio 538, Thieme Meulenhoff, Wolters Noordhoff, BNN, Wegener Nieuwsmedia, Ohra, KindaMuzik, Technisch Weekblad, Dutch Ministry of Justitie, Mangrove, Nationale Vereniging De Zonnebloem, NeonMob, Inspire Conference, Wakoopa.

History

Looking back, Bram has been drawing for as long as he can remember. He used to draw on telephone notebook since when he was a kid, but really picked it up around 10 years ago then evolves until now.

As most artists, Bram also started his artistic path at an art school but decided to drop out because it was not commercial enough for him at that time. In the beginning he always wanted to reach the audience because he knew how fun it would be based on his experience in a band.

"Art school really forces you to broad your verizon but it was too much theory, too much in crowd..." However if he could he would love to get the techniques down. However he also have his minor regret for not finishing it, one of the main things is because he was too young at that time.One of the reasons to go back to art school - start what you finish. He would love to see whats gonna happen if he did.

Style

Regarding style, Bram realizes that it evolves throughout the year. "It used to be crocked and weird; anatomically incorrect.. Like arms coming out of head", he explained.
