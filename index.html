Nozzman!

Introduction

If you're Dutch or living in the Netherlands, you most probably have seen some of his works sometime, somewhere unconsciously; Bram van Rijen, also known as Nozzman is a freelance graphic artist/illustrator/cartoonist and creative director at Mangrove in Rotterdam with Pop Surrealism genrea in his pocket.

Some of his clients are NRC Handelsblad, nrc.next, Veronica Magazine, Moleskine, Erasmus University, NAi, ING, Zone 5300, Radio 538, Thieme Meulenhoff, Wolters Noordhoff, BNN, Wegener Nieuwsmedia, Ohra, KindaMuzik, Technisch Weekblad, Dutch Ministry of Justitie, Mangrove, Nationale Vereniging De Zonnebloem, NeonMob, Inspire Conference, Wakoopa.

History

Looking back, Bram has always been creative as a kid, he has been drawing for as long as he can remember (telephone notebook was his creative canvas); however he (finally) really picked it up around 10 years ago then evolves until now.

How it started

As most artists, Bram started his artistic path at an art school (St. Joost in Breda) but decided to drop out because it was not commercial enough for him at that time. In the beginning he always wanted to reach the audience right away to experience the feedback, which was something he really enjoyed for being in a band.

Art school really forces you to broad your verizon but it was too much theory, too much in crowd...

As a change, he decided to jump into the field right away by putting his works online. At that time there was no serious competition online because most of the well known cartoonists did not have websites to represent their works yet; not only that, they did not feel too comfortable sharing their arts online (copyright infringement). That period gave Bram a good breathing space to explore the online world; in fact, he got his first commercial client called DVD Valley (which is now called Film Valley) approximately a year after that.

I think my problem was because I was too young.. I thought it was too much artful for art sake.

Intrigue by his story, I asked if he ever regretted not finishing his art degree. Admittedly, he has a minor regret for it; he would love to get the techniques down, to finish what he started and to see what would happen if he did. However, he (still) believes that what he's doing is actually a good balance for him. He acknowledges his passion was shifting throughout the years, he loves doing artsy projects more now than then.

Style

Regarding style, Bram realizes that it evolves throughout the year. "It used to be crocked and weird; anatomically incorrect.. Like arms coming out of head. These days they are more curvy", he explained.

For client works he always keep it fixed because it needs to be the certain style the client requested (particular comic style). They mostly have simplistic style because simple shapes help to tell stories. As an example, cartoon styles always implicate a funny message, it indicates humour yet the storyline can be a serious topic.

Inspirations

Finding inspirations is never an issue for Bram; in fact, he is never not inspired. The only thing that could get in the way is laziness and that has nothing to do with an artist's active state. One of the reasons can be the amount of hours he invests on his drawing books

Drawing book is very precious to him. It is his escape of freak out on diff styles force himself to not judge - just go not to sketch just go with shape - constantly evolves. enforce him to try new stuff.

There are many ways to train your imaginations, such as: a. Going to several musea. His favorite city is Paris, he loves going to several local artists' exhibitions) b. Creating your own books. His favorite artist is Robert Crumb. c. Movies such as Disney Pixar can ve a very good source of inspirations.Movies can be a really good inspirations, sometimes after watching a movie

Inspiration is overrated. It gives you an excuse to do nothing. The key is just get to it!
